# MCMC Sampling Issues, Targets, and Operational Rules

## Current MCMC Sampling Issues
The initial baseline run of the No-U-Turn Sampler (NUTS) using the JAX-differentiable waveform engine resulted in a catastrophic **Prior Boundary Pile-up**:
* **Chirp Mass ($M_c$):** Stuck exactly at the upper uniform prior bound of `50.0` with near-zero variance ($\text{sd} \approx 5 \times 10^{-15}$).
* **Luminosity Distance ($D$):** Stuck exactly at the upper uniform prior bound of `2000.0` with zero variance ($\text{sd} = 0.0$).
* **Corner Plot Crash:** The `corner.corner` plotting function crashed with `ValueError: It looks like the parameter(s) in column(s) 1 have no dynamic range` because there was zero variance in the luminosity distance posterior samples.
* **Extreme Divergences:** The sampler reported `2859 divergences after tuning` out of 5000 total post-tuning samples, indicating that the posterior surface is highly pathological or the model likelihood is physically misaligned.

## Operational Targets
* **Chirp Mass:** Recover a stable posterior distribution centered around the true GW150914 value ($\approx 28.3 M_\odot$) with realistic HDI bounds.
* **Luminosity Distance:** Recover a stable posterior distribution centered around the true GW150914 value ($\approx 410\text{ Mpc}$).
* **Convergence:** Eliminate divergences, achieve $\hat{R} < 1.01$, and ensure healthy effective sample sizes ($\text{ESS} > 100$ per chain).

# Debug Documentation

### 1. Physical & Numerical Diagnosis of the Boundary Pile-up
The catastrophic runaway of both $M_c$ and $D$ to their upper prior boundaries is a classic indicator of **Likelihood-Template Misalignment**. There are two separate layers to this issue:

#### A. Initial Layer: Lack of Template Whitening (Fixed in Step 2)
1. **The Observed Data is Whitened:** The input LIGO strain is processed via `raw_strain.whiten(fduration=2)`. This divides the frequency-domain data by the detector's Amplitude Spectral Density (ASD), $\sqrt{S_n(f)}$, flattening the noise power to a standard deviation of $\sigma = 1.0$.
2. **The Waveform Template was Unwhitened:** The JAX waveform generated by `_make_jax_waveform` is a raw general-relativistic waveform. Because it is not divided by the noise ASD, the unwhitened template contains massive low-frequency amplitudes that are orders of magnitude larger than the whitened data.
3. **The Sampler's Response:** Comparing whitened time-domain data to unwhitened time-domain templates in the likelihood ($\chi^2 = \sum (d_i - h_i)^2$) creates an enormous mathematical penalty. To minimize this, NUTS was forced to scale the template amplitude to zero by driving $D$ to its maximum upper bound (`2000.0` Mpc, since amplitude scales as $1/D$) and pushing $M_c$ to the prior boundaries.

#### B. Deeper Layer: Multi-modal Likelihood and Random Initialization (Active Issue)
1. **The Phase-Matching Challenge:** Gravitational wave templates are phase-coherent over hundreds of cycles. A shift of even $1$ millisecond ($0.001$ seconds) or a slight chirp mass mismatch ($0.5 M_\odot$) completely ruins the phase alignment, converting constructive interference into destructive interference.
2. **The Zero-Gradient Plateau:** Outside of an extremely narrow, multi-dimensional physical "well", the likelihood surface is completely flat (dominated by random background noise).
3. **Trapped in the Priors:** When NUTS is initialized randomly, it starts in this flat, zero-gradient region. To minimize the $\chi^2$ penalty of adding an unaligned template, the sampler immediately drives the template amplitude to zero by pushing the luminosity distance $D$ to its upper bound of `2000` Mpc. Once there, it wanders aimlessly in the flat, zero-gradient prior volume because no gradient exists to guide it back to the physical well.

1. **Continuous-to-Discrete Normalization Verification:**

This scales the time-domain template to the exact same units (noise standard deviations) as the whitened observed strain (amplitude max $\approx 5.47$ for the template vs $\approx 5.16$ for the data at $D = 410$ Mpc).
2. **JAX Gradient Verification:**
   By evaluating the sum-of-squares objective function (as a simple sum is mathematically zero for band-passed signals), we verified that JAX autodiff through the full `rippleGW` IMRPhenomD waveform is completely stable, finite, and non-zero:

### 3. Proposed Actionable Fixes
To prevent the sampler from getting trapped at the $D = 2000$ boundary and force it to explore the physical posterior mode, we propose the following implementation steps:

#### Fix 1: Informed Initialization
Instead of random initialization, we must initialize the NUTS chains inside the physical well. Pass the optimized best-fit values as starting coordinates to the sampling function:
```python
initvals = {
    "chirp_mass": 28.3,
    "luminosity_distance": 870.0,
    "tc": 0.5295
}
# Pass initvals to pm_jax.sample_numpyro_nuts(...)
```

#### Fix 2: Add Coalescence Phase Prior ($\phi_c$)
Do not hardcode $\phi_c = 0.0$ in `_make_jax_waveform`. Add $\phi_c$ as an active parameter with a uniform prior:
```python
phic = pm.Uniform("phic", lower=-np.pi, upper=np.pi)
```
This allows the sampler to find the phase-coherent fit, maximizing the likelihood.

#### Fix 3: Targeted, Physical Priors
To restrict the sampler from wandering into the flat noise-only prior volume, narrow down the prior ranges around the physical signal window:
- **Chirp Mass ($M_c$):** `lower=20.0, upper=35.0` (Standard Uniform prior)
- **Luminosity Distance ($D$):** `lower=200.0, upper=1500.0`
- **Coalescence Time ($t_c$):** `lower=0.51, upper=0.55` (A narrow, safe 40 ms window around the known merger time)
